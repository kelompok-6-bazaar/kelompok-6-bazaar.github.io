BAB 1

ABSTRAK

The Integrated Learning (IL) Project was organized as a bazaar and performance event, which was designed to combine multiple subjects into a hands-on, real-world experience. This project was meant to help students develop skills like teamwork, marketing, budgeting, and problem-solving. The theme for this year’s project was chosen as “Nusantara,” and our class decided to focus on Indonesian movies. Our group was inspired by the popular film “Dilan 1990”, and we named our booth “Pojok Dilan.”

The products we sold were carefully selected to match the nostalgic vibe of the movie. Initially, yogurt bites and cireng were considered, but they were later dropped due to their complexity. Instead, we decided to sell chicken satay, fried noodle spring rolls, crispy tempe sticks (for biotech), kue cubit, iced tea, keychains, stickers, batik fans, and bracelets. The prices were set to be affordable for students while still making a profit. Product trials were conducted at home because group meetings were limited due to conflicting schedules.

For the booth design, Enji was assigned to create the initial sketches. However, the final design was adjusted due to budget constraints. Decorations featuring Dilan and Milea were added to enhance the theme.The logo and sticker designs were revised multiple times to meet the group’s preferences. A promotional video and posters were created to encourage pre-orders, which were opened in two batches due to initial delays.

During the bazaar, several challenges were faced. Booth decorations were adjusted multiple times because they kept falling apart. The biotech product, tempeh, was particularly challenging to make because it required fermentation, a process we had no experience with. Despite these difficulties, the bazaar was executed smoothly, and our products were sold successfully.

Through this project, we learned the importance of teamwork, creativity, and adaptability. We also gained a deeper appreciation for Indonesian culture and the value of promoting local products. The IL Project was a great opportunity to apply classroom knowledge in a practical setting and to develop skills that will help us in the future.
